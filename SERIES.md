# Series Map

## Structuralism
- **Purpose**: Introduce structuralism as a method for identifying systems of relations beneath visible cultural forms.
- **Why it matters**: It shifts attention from isolated objects to organizing rules, which helps explain language, myth, institutions, and ideology.
- **Likely lecture arc**:
  1. What structuralism tries to see
  2. Language and relational meaning
  3. Myth, kinship, and social form
  4. Critiques and post-structural turns

## Phenomenology
- **Purpose**: Clarify phenomenology as a disciplined description of experience from the first-person standpoint.
- **Why it matters**: It offers tools for analyzing perception, embodiment, intention, and lifeworld without reducing them to abstraction.
- **Likely lecture arc**:
  1. Method and epoché
  2. Intentionality and world-relation
  3. Body, time, and intersubjectivity
  4. Links to existential and critical traditions

## Capital
- **Purpose**: Build a clear conceptual path into capital as social relation, process, and historical structure.
- **Why it matters**: It supports analysis of labor, value, crisis, and contemporary inequality in a non-superficial way.
- **Likely lecture arc**:
  1. Capital beyond money
  2. Labor, value, and abstraction
  3. Accumulation and contradiction
  4. Contemporary reinterpretations

# Structuralism Overview

## Working purpose
Build a lecture sequence that explains structuralism as a mode of analysis focused on relations, rules, and systems rather than isolated entities.

## Why this theme comes first
Structuralism is a strong first test theme because it demonstrates how method changes what we can see. It also creates a bridge to phenomenology and critiques of capitalism by clarifying how meaning and social order are patterned.

## Core questions
- What does structuralism claim we miss when we focus only on individual objects or events?
- How does relational meaning work in language and culture?
- Where are the strengths and limits of structural analysis?

## Planned arc (initial)
1. **Episode 01**: What Structuralism Tries to See
2. Language, sign, and relational meaning
3. Myth and kinship as structured systems
4. Critiques and transitions toward post-structural approaches

## Working cautions
- Do not reduce structuralism to a single thinker.
- Distinguish methodological claims from historical applications.
- Keep examples concrete before abstract generalization.

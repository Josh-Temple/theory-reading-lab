# Structuralism Research Notes

## Source-based claims (to preserve)
- In Saussurean linguistics, signification is differential: terms gain value through relations, not intrinsic content.
- Structural analysis often prioritizes synchronic relations before diachronic explanation.
- Lévi-Strauss extends relational analysis to myth and kinship by modeling transformations and oppositions.

## Interpretive claims (working)
- Structuralism is best treated as a method family, not a single doctrine.
- Its strongest contribution is analytic: making implicit rules visible.
- Its weakest point appears when relational models are used with insufficient historical mediation.

## Teaching simplifications (allowed, but monitor)
- "Look for the system under the surface" is a useful entry line for Episode 01.
- "Meaning by difference" is a compact phrase for first exposure.
- Use one language example and one social-practice example before introducing critiques.

## Open questions
- How far can linguistic models travel into social analysis without distortion?
- How should Episode 04 frame critiques without caricaturing structuralism?

## To verify with sources

## Episode 01 extraction (first pass)
- Structuralism starts from relations among elements, not isolated units.
- Meaning is differential: value comes from contrasts inside a system.
- Method first, total theory second: use structural analysis as a lens, then test limits.
